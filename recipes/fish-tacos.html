Fish Tacos

Fish tacos is a staple found within the hispanic culture street food. They are also a popular for beach side locations and summer time grill outs. Here's a take one can do from the comfort of their own home to experience this dish!

Ingredients


Beer Batter
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour

  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

  • 1 teaspoon baking powder

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • 1 cup beer

  • 1 egg

White Sauce
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise

  • 1 lime, juiced

  • 1 jalapeno pepper, minced

  • 1 teaspoon minced capers

  • 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper

  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano

  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin

  • 1/2 teaspoon dried dill weed

Fish Tacos
  • 1 quart oil for frying

  • 1 pound cod fillets, cut into 2 to 3 ounce portions

  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour, or more as needed

  • 1 (12 ounce) package corn tortillas

  • 1/2 medium head cabbage, finely shredded

Steps

  1. Make beer batter: Combine flour, cornstarch, baking powder, & salt in a large bowl. Blend beer & egg in a separate bowl, then quickly stir into flour mixture until combined with a few lumps remaining.

  2. Make white sauce: Mix together yogurt & mayonnaise in a medium bowl. Gradually stir in fresh lime juice until consistency is slightly runny. Season with jalapeno, capers, cayenne, oregano, cumin, and dill.

  3. Start fish tacos: Heat oil in a deep-fryer to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C)

  4. Dust fish pieces lightly with flour. Set aside.

  5. Dip floured fish pieces into beer batter. Set aside

  6. Fry in hot oil until crisp & golden brown. Drain on paper towels. Lightly fry tortillas in hot oil until just crisped, but not too crisp. Drain on paper towels.

  7. Place fried fish in tortillas; top with shredded cabbage & white sauce.
